This was one of the first coding projects I did. The instruction was to design a website with our classmates as target group. The application should solve a problem the target group has or make their life easier and should be responsive, made with (at least) HTML and CSS. The idea of the application had to be clear, but it didn't have to be programmed.
I made an application called 'Snooze' which was designed for students - such as me - who are always late. The idea was that you could download your school scedule into the app, give the app your current location and tell the app how much time you need in the morning, so that the app would figure out what time to wake you.


This webapplication is made with HTML, CSS and JavaScript.

The assignment was to make a portfolio to display what has been made and the skills that have been acquired.
The lettertype "Source Code Pro" is used for headers, to give the website a 'coding' feel and the lettertype "Adelle Sans" is used for paragraphs, to give the website a minimalistic, clear feel. The background in the wireframes isn't that important yet. It's there to show that the menu and content of the website will have a white, partly transparant background. The homepage of the website would be the most important asset of the website as it is the guide to show the viewer what this website contains, plus it will be the first thing the viewer sees when they look up the website. That is why the logo is very big and in the center of the homepage. It is a first introduction to the website and their maker.


These wireframes were made with Adobe XD
